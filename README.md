# DAFSEG
Segmenting building damage from satellite images following a disaster is crucial to expedite humanitarian response and infrastructure recovery. Despite progress, the majority of current methods struggle with addressing class imbalance, visual ambiguity, and weak feature interaction across diverse disaster categories, thereby limiting their practical utility. To overcome these challenges, we introduce a novel dual-stream segmentation framework that independently encodes pre-disaster and post-disaster satellite images utilizing parallel ResNet50 backbones. A key component of the proposed architecture is the BestFusionSelector, an adaptive fusion module that dynamically chooses among concatenation, subtraction, and attention-based pathways to generate context-aware fused features tailored to each input. These fused representations are decoded using a DeepLabV3 segmentation head to provide pixel-level, four-class damage maps: Background, Intact, Damaged, and Destroyed. Experiments using the BRIGHT benchmark, spanning seven global disaster types, demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method. The proposed model achieves a 63.17\% mean Intersection over Union (mIoU), a 93.85\% overall pixel accuracy, and a macro F1-score of 75.53\%. Notably, improved performance is observed for the deestroyed class, highlighting enhanced sensitivity to severe damage patterns under severe class imbalance.
Overall, the proposed pipeline DAFseg is interpretable, reproducible, and scalable. It offers a practical solution for real-world post-disaster damage assessment and a strong baseline for future research in multimodal remote sensing.
